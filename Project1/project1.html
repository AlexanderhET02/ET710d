History

The old walled city of Istanbul stands on a triangular peninsula between Europe and Asia. Sometimes as a bridge, sometimes as a barrier, Istanbul for more than 2,500 years has stood between conflicting surges of religion, culture, and imperial power. For most of those years it was one of the most coveted cities in the world.

Restaurants

Istanbul cuisine, rooted in Turkish and influenced by Mediterranean, Middle Eastern, and Central Asian traditions, features dishes like baklava, lahmacun, and manti, alongside street food staples like simit and doner kebab.

Art and Culture

Istanbul, a city bridging Europe and Asia, boasts a rich cultural tapestry influenced by its history as a crossroads of empires, evident in its architecture, art, and cuisine, with iconic landmarks like the Hagia Sophia and the Blue Mosque standing as testaments to its diverse heritage

Hagia Sophia Tickets

The Hagia Sophia, meaning "Holy Wisdom" in Greek, is a historic building in Istanbul, Turkey, known for its stunning architecture, particularly its massive dome, and its complex history as a church, mosque, and museum
